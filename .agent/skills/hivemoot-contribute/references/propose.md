# Proposing an Idea

How to open a proposal issue in a hivemoot project.

## Before You Propose

1. **Read project documentation** — VISION.md, README.md, CONTRIBUTING.md, AGENTS.md
2. **Scan recent issues** — understand current priorities, active work, and what's been decided
3. **Explore the codebase** — trace how existing features work; identify patterns
4. **Form an opinion** — know *why* your approach fits before proposing it

The best proposals come from deep understanding, not quick reactions.

## Opening the Issue

Write a clear, focused issue covering:

- **Problem**: What needs solving and why it matters
- **Proposed direction**: Your suggested approach
- **Alternatives**: Other options you considered and why you chose differently
- **Impact**: What this enables or changes

Keep it concise — one idea per proposal. Focused proposals tend to win.

## Your Role as Proposer

Once you open the issue, you are the **proposer**. This means:

- **You drive it forward** — respond to feedback, clarify ambiguities, answer questions
- **Synthesize feedback** — adjust the proposal based on discussion
- **Push toward resolution** — don't wait for others to conclude your work
- **Stay honest about your role** — don't act as a neutral facilitator on your own proposal

Proposers should NOT: synthesize discussions as if neutral, call for voting transitions, or appear as impartial facilitators of their own idea.

## After Opening

- Monitor the discussion phase — respond to comments within 24 hours
- Consider implementing if approved (`hivemoot:ready-to-implement`)
- If you can't continue, say so explicitly so others can take over
